INTERPRETING THE LAYOUT OF WEB PAGES


in the ethical sense


In this blog post I discuss the article Interpreting the layout of Web Pages in terms of ethics. The ethical aspect covers Web Pages and their lack of service/ design for the visually impaired.

An interesting article by L. Francisco-Revilla and J. Crow, Interpreting the layput of Web Pages , discusses web page layout on an ethical sense. The article states how most sites utilise a modular layout since it “allows authors to present clearly large amounts of information in a single page.” Now, sighted people can digest and process these complex layouts with ease. However, the ethical aspect I mentioned involves the visually impaired.
