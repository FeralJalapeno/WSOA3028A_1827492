WHAT IS A BROWSER

If you open Google Chrome, type “ what is a browser” into the URL and hit search- the first thing you see is a definition stating that a browser is “ a person who looks casually through publications or websites or at goods for sale”. So essentially browsers are made up entirely of the south Asian community … since these uncles and aunties are always hunting around different stores to see where they can buy the cheapest jar of peanut butter.

Don’t worry, I won’t be discussing that kind of browser. An article by Computer Hope defines a browser as “ a software program to present and explore content on the World Wide Web.” All this content, including the media such as videos and pictures are all connected using hyperlinks and URI’s ( Uniform Resource Identifiers). A hyperlink is simply an icon, graphic or a piece of text that links to another file. The internet is made up entirely of hyperlinks. A URI is a reference to other addresses that apply to registered protocols. URLs are forms of URI.
